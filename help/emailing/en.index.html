Creating an email campaign

using the Eudonet Email Builder

Good practices to get the most out of the email builder

  • The maximum width of an email must be between 600px and 800px. This will ensure a better display of your email on small screens and when previewing the email in mailboxes.
  • Avoid complex HTML elements and use a grid structure rather than elements with fixed or relative positions as they may not position correctly on all email providers.
  • Most email systems by default block the display of images. It is therefore necessary to provide alternative texts for images ("Alt") and avoid using a background image for the email.
  • To improve the deliverability and display of the email, it is advisable not to have only one image containing all the information or to have only images without text in the email. The ideal ratio is 60% text and 40% image.
  • When designing your email use fonts that are compatible with as many email providers as possible, such as Arial, Verdana, Georgia or Times New Roman.
  • When constructing your email avoid content that requires Flash or Javascript as it is not that well supported by all email providers and opt for animated .gif instead.
  • The use of media features helps to improve visibility and accessibility on small screens and mobiles, reorganising images and texts, increasing the size of text and buttons to make them easier to read and click from a mobile. By building your email from the blocks we offer, you automatically benefit from these media features.
  • Test your email before sending your campaign:
    • Using the "Send a Test Email" function to test the display and responsiveness of the email in different mailboxes / different screens sizes as well as via a mobile device.
    • You can use services like www.mail-tester.com to obtain a rating for your email. These services take into account several parameters such as the validity of the links present, sending information and the proportion of the images/texts.
    • Test your email, selecting yourself as the only recipient of your email campaign.
  • Always test your email one last time before sending it.

Note

To benefit from all the email creation features, we advise that you use one of the following browsers Chrome, Firefox or Safari.

Note

When entering a colour in hexcodes, make sure to prefix "#" so that your colour is correctly interpreted by all email providers.

Step 1: Choosing the template

    You can choose a template from:

  • Your templates: This list contains the emails you have created and those that other users have shared with you.
  • Customisable templates: Here you will find a list of responsive templates that have been made available to you in the hope that you will find some inspiration.
  • Basic template: Choose this option if you want to create a simple email or if you want to start with a blank page.

Note

The "Basic template" has a width of 680px and is left-aligned and the "Centered Email" model has a body width of 680px centered.

Top Tip

To start creating a new centered email, choose "Centered Email" from the list of customisable templates.

The "Text" block

You can drag and drop a "Text" block directly into the email builder so that you can then edit it.

You can then edit its content using the layout options that appear in the bar at the top of the builder.

Note

To modify the height of the "Text" block, click in the empty space outside the Text but in the inside of the "Text" Block. The menu on the right-hand side will then display the properties of the block, open the Dimension and margins option and you can then modify the height property.

Note

To modify the inner margins of the text block, click in the text box. The menu on the right-hand side will then display the properties of the block, in the Dimension and margins option. You can then modify the inner margin property.

The "Image" block

You can drag an "Image" block directly into the email builder so that you can then edit this element of the email.

You then can double click on the default image to open the editing menu and select your image to upload.

Top Tip

An alternative text can be defined in the image settings (Double-click on the image). It is strongly recommended to define an alternative text ("Alt") on your images, this is a brief description of the images in your email that is displayed when the images are not accessible. Some email providers disable images by default, so the alternative text indicates the value of the image to your recipient. This alternative text is also useful for the accessibility of your email for recipients who use screen reading software. Also, the presence of alternative text is part of good practices for the deliverability of your email.

Top Tip

You can use the button below to view the rendering of your email when the images are not accessible by the email provider.

Top Tip

It is strongly recommended to use images that already have the dimensions you want for your email. You can force the width and height in the image settings (Double-click on the image) but some mailboxes do not take these settings into account.

The "Button" block

The "Button" block allows you to add a call to action button to your email. To add this feature, you can drag a "Button" block into your email.

Note

Links are defined in step 3 - Content and links.

A button with a default formatting is displayed.

Change the colour of the button

Select the button by clicking in the white area inside the button. The menu on the righthand side will then display the button properties. In the option "Colour and border", the option "Background colour" will allow you to change the background colour of the button.

Border radius

This allows you to define the curvature of the button angles. This feature is not compatible with Outlook email.

Border

This allows you to define the width, style, and colour of the borders. (Note: To remove the button borders, choose "none" Style).

Change the buttons text colour

Click in the text box inside the button to edit its content: a layout toolbar is displayed at the top of the email and allows you to change the layout of the text.

Change the width of the button

Select the button by clicking in the white area inside the button, the menu on the right-hand side will then display the button properties. Open the Dimension and margins option and this will then allow you to modify the width.

Change the height of the button

Select the button by clicking in the white area inside the button, the menu on the right-hand side will then display the button properties. Open the Dimension and margin option this will then allow you to change the High and Low margin properties.

Change the buttons block height

Select the button by clicking in the white area inside the button, the menu on the right-hand side will then display the button properties, in the Colour and border option, these options allow you to change the appearance of the button borders.

Change the button borders

Select the button by clicking in the white area inside the button, the menu on the right-hand side will then display the button properties, in the Colour and border option, these options allow you to change the appearance of the button borders.

The "Separator" block

The "Separator" block allows you to visually divide the content of your email.

Change the height of the separator

Select the separator by clicking on it, the menu on the right-hand side will then display the properties of the separator. In the Dimension and margin option, the Height option (default 5px) allows you to change the height of the separator.

Change the colour of the separator

Select the separator by clicking on it, the menu on the right-hand side will then display the properties of the separator. In the colour and border option, select the background colour option this will allow you to change the background colour of the button.

Change the separator margins

Select the separator by clicking on it, then open the Dimension and margin option. By then going up to the HTML element with a default top margin of 5px, you can modify the top and bottom margins of this HTML element.

The "Article" block

The Article block allows you to add to your email a block consisting of an image, a title, a text box, and a button, each element can be modified as if it were a single block.

The "Column" blocks

Block "1 Column" It allows you to create a free area in your email where you can define a background colour and then drop other blocks (Image, Text, Article...) into the email.
Block "2 Columns" It allows you to have two areas on the same line to then drop other blocks (Image, Text, Article...) into the email.
Block "2 Columns 30% / 70%" It allows you to have two areas on the same line to then drop other blocks into with a smaller column on the left-hand side compared to the right-hand side.

Top Tip

For "2 column" blocks if you want your elements to be vertically aligned, you need to define each element to have an identical height corresponding to the largest of the 2 elements.

The "Layout" Blocks

"Header" Block This block contains a visualisation link, to view the email in a web browser.
"Body" Block This block contains a "Text" block that can be modified.
"Footer" Block This block contains, on the left-hand side, the contact details to customise. The right-hand side contains information such as web links and social networks. Below contains the link for managing subscriptions as well as your personalised links to social networks which are to be filled in at the "Content and links" step.

Top Tip

Select the central cell and change the "Width" parameter within the "Dimensions and margins" option to change the width of the inner block from the builder’s default setting of "680px", to the size you have set for the email width.

Top Tip

Select the background cell and change the "Background colour" parameter of the "Colour and border" option to change the background colour of this block, this allows you to have a global background colour for the email and a different colour for the footer block only.

Top Tip

In the "Footer" block you can also remove a social media network, by selecting the cell and clicking on the "Delete" button.

You can also replace the proposed social media networks by double-clicking on the image to replace it and then change the name of the link.

Note

CSS properties on the body (body {}) are not supported by the email editor.

Note

The CSS present in the source of your email is passed inline. Some mailboxes do not take into account the CSS code defined in the <style> tags of your code, that is why Eudonet X transforms your CSS code into inline to make the display of your code more accurate in the different mailboxes and emailing providers.

Note

To modify the CSS style sheet, you can open the CSS code of your email via the "CSS" button in the "Contents and links" step.

The options available on a selected HTML element

Duplicate Duplicates the selected HTML element and its content (Be careful, you must go back to the correct level of the HTML structure to duplicate the table and not just the cell or line).
Delete Deletes the selected HTML element and its content (Warning, you must go back to the correct level of the HTML structure to delete the table and not just the cell or line).

Under Firefox, the colour selection window is set by default with an opacity of 0%. For the colour to be displayed, move the transparency slider to the right.

Step 3: Content and links

    This step allows you to use all the layout features within the builder to edit your email text, insert merge fields and define redirect links.

Note

Inserting tables or images outside the graphic creation stage is not recommended because it no longer ensures the responsive design of the email you are constructing.

The links

Before selecting one of these options, select the text on which the link will be placed.

Link to an attachment This function allows you to upload an attachment from your computer to the server and place a download link in the email.
Link to a URL This option allows you to place a link to an external URL in the email.
Unsubscribe link & subscription management This option allows you to place a link that will allow the recipient to unsubscribe or manage their subscriptions according to the unsubscribe management mode. (Note: this link is not functional when sending test emails)

Top Tip

Blocks containing text (Blocks "Text", "Article", "Header"...) do not have a predefined font, the default font of the email is defined in the .css style sheet and can be modified in the step "Content and links" via the CSS display button.


Note

This style sheet will be included in the <HEAD> tag of your email.

Link tracking

Link

When you place a link in your email, you can set up these tracking options.

Tracking options

  • Disable tracking link: This option allows you not to track click statistics on this link.
  • Link name: Name the link to improve its accessibility and make it easier to find the associated statistics.
  • Link field: The "Link field" option allows you to define the field of the Target/Guest/Recipient form that will be updated when the email recipient clicks on that link. Two types of fields are available:
    • Checkbox: The checkbox from the first click on the link.
    • Numerical: The value of the numerical field is incremented by 1 with each click.

Step 4: Sending the email

    At this stage, you can:

  • Give a description of your emailing campaign.
  • Count the number of recipients.
  • Define the expiry and purge date of track links.
  • Define the immediate or scheduled send options.
  • Choose to duplicate search the sending on email addresses.

Click send and that is it the campaign is now sent.

That's it, your campaign is sent! You can analyse the statistics of your campaign in the "E-mail Campaigns" tab within Eudonet. This will allow you to follow the clicks on the tracking’s links and the answers to the Eudonet forms on the Recipients/Target/Guest.
